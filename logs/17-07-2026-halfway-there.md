## What I Learned

Today marked an important milestone—I completed **50% of Striver's A2Z DSA Sheet**.

Wrapping up the Sliding Window and Two Pointers module feels satisfying because the pattern is finally becoming intuitive. Instead of mechanically following steps, I can often visualize the window moving, know when it needs to shrink, and understand what information needs to be maintained. There are still exceptions, though. Problems like **Minimum Window Substring** remind me that there's always another layer to learn, and that's one I'll definitely revisit.

I also spent more time with DOM manipulation. Learning selectors, and how to create, edit, and remove elements made me feel much more comfortable with how JavaScript interacts with a webpage. Tomorrow, I want to put that knowledge into practice by building a few small projects instead of just reading about the APIs.

Another fun discovery today was **Streamlit**. Most of my ML projects have lived inside Jupyter notebooks, and Streamlit feels like the missing piece that can turn them into something people can actually interact with. Rather than simply giving an old project a new UI, I think it'd be more exciting to find an interesting dataset, experiment with it, and build a complete Streamlit app from scratch.

Today felt less like finishing things and more like opening doors to what I can build next.
