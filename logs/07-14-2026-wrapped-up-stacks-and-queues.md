# Wrapped Up Stacks and Queues

**Date**: 2026-07-14
**Topic**: Stacks, Queues, Monotonic Stack, LRU Cache

## What I Learned
I've finally wrapped up the Stacks & Queues module.

Compared to the previous topics, I found this one a lot more challenging—not because the concepts were difficult, but because many of the problems weren't straightforward. Monotonic stack questions, implementation-heavy problems, and cache design problems really made me slow down and think.

Instead of trying to memorize patterns or solutions, I spent most of my time doing dry runs, asking why a particular data structure worked, and trying multiple approaches before looking for hints. My goal was to understand the thought process behind the pattern rather than remember individual questions.

There are still a couple of topics that I don't feel completely comfortable with yet, especially LRU Cache and LFU Cache. They make sense after solving them, but I know they're the kind of problems that need revisiting every now and then. I'll definitely come back to them later.

Another thing of note is that dealing with this module made me look at stacks and queues more as a concept and less as a rigid collection framework. I used arraylists, linkedlists, deque which functioned as stacks or queues depending on the demand of the question. This is one aspect which I want to explore further with other data structures too.

Overall, this module taught me that sometimes progress isn't about solving a large number of questions—it's about spending time with the difficult ones until the pattern starts to make sense.

Next up: Sliding Window and Two Pointers. Looking forward to seeing what new patterns they introduce.
